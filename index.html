History

1774 - The Beginning

Langen-Bergheim in Hesses entered in the church archives in the city of Langen-Bergheim in Hesse as a "shoe maker and subject"

1876 - The Footbed is Born

Langen-Bergheim in'the great-great-grandson of Johann Adam Birkenstock) had a visionary idea: make and sell flexible footbed inserts. From there, it wasn't long before he invented the footbed.

Although the soles of our feet are curved, shoes had always been flat inside. Konrad theorized that if the shape of the shoes reflected the shape of the feet, greater comfort would be possible. He designs the first shoe with a contoured insole to be used by shoemakers in the production of custom footwear. He then went on to develop the first flexible arch support insert, designed to be inserted into factory-made shoes.
